

**1. Definition and Core Concept of ODS**

- **What it is:** Online Digital Storage (also known as **Cloud Computing**) uses a service provider to store data online, avoiding the need to store data locally on physical disks.
- **Purpose:** It acts as an add-on method for existing data storage solutions and provides better access for an increasing number of people.
- **Examples:** Popular ODS platforms include Google Drive, Dropbox, OneDrive, iCloud, Amazon Web Services (AWS), Netflix, and Facebook.

**2. Pros and Cons of ODS**

- **Advantages:** Great for storing personal documents and media files, keeping backup copies, and providing easy access for people on the move.
- **Disadvantages:** Highly dependent on internet speeds, comes with limited storage space (unless paid), incurs service charges, and suffers from a lack of security.

**3. Threats to Permanence (Crucial for Data Survival)**

- **Application Obsolescence:** As old technology is replaced, materials created on outdated technology become inaccessible if not converted to compatible new formats (e.g., digital archives disappearing when a news outlet goes out of business).
- **Corruption:** Digital materials are made up of "bits." If even one bit is corrupted or altered, it can result in the entire record's effective death.
- **Completeness:** Records need **metadata** (like file name, creation date, modification history) to remain comprehensible. This metadata should be kept in a manifest or spreadsheet so future users know a file's purpose.
- **Findability:** Files are essentially lost and useless if they cannot be located. Employing conscientious and consistent file-naming customs is necessary to organize and find records.

**4. Cybersecurity, Privacy, and Selecting an ODS**

- **Security Risks:** The slides highlight massive privacy infringements, including the leak of 533 million Facebook users' personal data, the sale of 4 million Malaysian citizens' data (from JPN/myIDENTITY), and international hacking incidents.
- **Privacy Laws:** Data privacy is heavily tied to regulations like Europe's **GDPR** (General Data Protection Regulation).
- **Criteria for Choosing an ODS:** When selecting a provider, you must:
    - Thoroughly check the contract text before agreeing.
    - Ensure you have exclusive ownership of the account.
    - Find out exactly where the physical storage facilities are located.
    - Always use unique usernames and access passwords.
