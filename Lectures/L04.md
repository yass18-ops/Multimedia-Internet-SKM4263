
**1. The Role and Purpose of Photo Archives**

- **Definition:** Photo archives are collections of images that can be public, private, academic, commercial, or historical.
- **Function:** Their main purpose is to enliven web pages and keep readers lingering on a site longer.

**2. Key Archiving Tools and Their Distinctive Features**

- Archiving tools (like Dropbox or Photobucket) generally host your images for free and provide HTML links to embed photo galleries directly onto websites.
- **Flickr:** Recognized as an early pioneer of Web 2.0 that revolutionized photo storage and introduced social bookmarking via tags.
- **Instagram:** A mobile photo-sharing app famous for its digital filters, social bookmarking via hashtags, and its distinctive square-shaped photos (prior to August 2015).
- **Facebook:** A massive repository that sees roughly 350 million photo uploads per day (as of 2019) and utilizes friend tags for social bookmarking.

**3. Copyright, Ownership, and NFTs**

- **The Fine Print:** When you upload photos to platforms like Facebook or Instagram, the platforms do not claim outright ownership. However, by uploading, you grant them a **non-exclusive, fully paid, royalty-free, transferable, sub-licensable, worldwide license** to use your content, and you remain legally liable for what you post.
- **NFTs (Non-Fungible Tokens):** These act as blockchain-protected certificates of ownership for digital assets. While some internet memes have earned massive payouts as NFTs, the trend has also led to rampant art theft where creators' works are minted without permission.

**4. Advancements in Image Recognition**

- Technology has progressed from identifying faces (Facebook Faces) and emotions (Cortana) to recognizing individuals by their specific walking patterns (Southampton University Gait).
- **Ethical Concerns:** Despite these advancements, a US government study has confirmed that most facial recognition systems exhibit racist biases.

**5. AI Generators, Deepfakes, and Verifying Authenticity**

- **AI Boom:** Generative AI tools like Midjourney, DALL-E, Gemini, and ChatGPT are taking the world by storm, but they introduce severe privacy nightmares and vague data deletion policies.
- **Deepfakes:** Manipulated AI videos are becoming a dangerous global problem. Detection tools like Deepware and FotoForensics are necessary to combat this.
- **Verification Methods:** You can verify an image's authenticity by looking at its **EXIF data**, conducting **Reverse Image Searches**, or using **ELA (Error Level Analysis)**.
- **Spotting Fake News:** Audiences must be trained to critically evaluate content by checking the source, the author, the date, supporting sources, and their own internal biases.
