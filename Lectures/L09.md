

**1. Types and Capabilities of Multimedia AI**
*   **Core Areas:** AI in multimedia is categorized into four main types: Image and Design, Video Production, Audio and Music, and Copywriting. 
*   **Transformative Capabilities:** AI enables advanced generation methods such as text-to-image, image-to-video, text-to-video, and text-to-audio. 

**2. Constraints of Generative AI**
*   Despite its power, Generative AI is limited by several technical and systemic constraints:
    *   **Engines & Styles:** Output depends heavily on the specific engine (e.g., DALL-E, Imagen, Stable Diffusion) and styles/presets (e.g., Anime, Photo-realistic).
    *   **Limitations:** Users face restrictions regarding **quotas** (free vs. paid capacity limits), **queue times** (fast vs. slow rendering), **guardrails** (safety filters), and **upscaling** (resolution limits for prints).

**3. Specialized AI Tools in Action**
*   **Video & Editing:** Deepfake technology (face-swapping) and auto-editors like CapCut, Veed, and Google Vids are making complex video production significantly easier.
*   **Audio & Music:** AI is being used heavily for **Voice Cloning** (e.g., ElevenLabs) and **Music Generation** (e.g., Suno).
*   **Real-World Usage:** AI is utilized to create hyper-realistic visuals, such as editing lion manes for keratin shampoo ads, or generating dramatic, movie-poster-style composites.

**4. Severe Ethical Concerns and Dangers**
*   **The Deepfake Threat:** Deepfakes are causing major real-world harm, including political manipulation (e.g., targeting Ex-Singapore president Halimah ahead of elections) and enabling massive financial fraud (causing an estimated $200 million in losses in 2025 alone).
*   **Security & Safety Dangers:** The slides highlight alarming incidents, such as an employee's AI tool download exposing Disney to a major security breach, Google's AI allegedly fueling a user's delusional spiral, and Microsoft developing an AI voice synthesizer that was deemed "too dangerous to release". 

**5. Copyright, Ownership, and the "AI vs. Humans" Debate**
*   **Artistic Backlash:** The use of original art to train AI without consent has caused outrage. Studio Ghibli co-founder Hayao Miyazaki strongly rejected the technology as an "insult to life itself," and many original artists feel violated when their work is fed into AI models. 
*   **The Ultimate Takeaway:** Even though AI art has advanced enough to win state fair competitions, the overarching conclusion is that **AI complements human creativity** rather than replacing it entirely. Moving forward, artists and designers **must learn to co-create with AI tools**.
