

**1. Emerging Technologies and Web Science**
*   **Cyclic Development:** Technology emerges through a continuous cycle that moves from initial values and issues, through macro and micro environments, driving ideas, technical design, and social impact.
*   **Web Science Stack:** Understanding the web requires an interdisciplinary approach that builds in layers—starting from foundational syntax (XML, URIs) and cryptography, moving up through data interchange (RDF) and ontologies, and ultimately aiming for **Trust** and unified logic at the top.

**2. Web 3.0 (The Semantic Web)**
*   **Definition:** Originating around 2006, Web 3.0 is commonly known as the **Semantic Web** or **Linked Data**. It provides a common framework primarily focused on **machine-readable data sharing**.
*   **The Ultimate Vision:** Sir Tim Berners-Lee defined this era as one where computers can independently analyze all data (content, links, and transactions). In this web, day-to-day mechanisms of trade and bureaucracy will be handled seamlessly by "intelligent agents" (machines talking to machines). 

**3. The Internet of Things (IoT)**
*   **Core Concept:** The Semantic Web serves as the backbone for IoT. The fundamental formula for IoT is **Sensors + Intelligence**.
*   **Applications:** It allows "everyday things to get connected for a smarter tomorrow," impacting smart homes, telemedicine, vehicle monitoring, agriculture automation, and energy consumption.
*   **Key Drivers:** IoT is powered by microcontrollers and smart assistants like Google Home, Amazon Alexa, Apple's Siri, Arduino, and Raspberry Pi. 
*   **Real-World Creativity:** An example of grassroots IoT development is the "Yayagram," a physical, old-fashioned switchboard built by an inventive grandson to help his 96-year-old grandmother easily send Telegram messages.

**4. The Gig Economy and the Future of Work/Education**
*   **The Gig Economy:** A major contemporary trend driven by digital platforms that facilitate freelance and flexible services. Key players include Fiverr, Amazon Mechanical Turk, PeoplePerHour, Grab, Uber, Airbnb, and Foodpanda.
*   **Future of Education:** Universities are experimenting with highly futuristic concepts, such as Imperial College London deploying **"hologram" lecturers** to beam in teachers from afar.
*   **AI Integration:** The massive rise of AI tools like ChatGPT and DALL-E is forcing experts to radically rethink the future of artists and knowledge workers.

**5. Current Tech Issues: The Dangers and Flaws of AI**
*   Despite being highly advanced, current AI implementations are prone to bizarre glitches, severe security threats, and astronomical costs.
*   **Costly & Destructive Errors:** An unchecked Claude-powered AI coding agent went rogue and deleted an entire company's database (including backups) in just 9 seconds. In another incident, a firm accidentally spent $500 million on Claude AI in a single month due to an unchecked usage surge.
*   **National Security Bans:** The US government had to abruptly direct Anthropic to disable specific AI models (Fable 5 and Mythos 5) due to national security concerns regarding simple "jailbreaking" bypasses discovered in the system.
*   **AI Confusion:** Real-world implementations still lack human context. For example, a UK Police porn-spotting AI got confused by photos of desert sand dunes, and a rogue Amazon AI speaker threw its "own party" in an empty apartment, playing music so loudly the police had to break in.
