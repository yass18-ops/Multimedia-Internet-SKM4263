
**1. Types of Online Multimedia Content**

- **Content Networks:** These are closed systems with strict checks and balances, typically driven by editors, in-house staff, and writers. Examples include Astro, Malay Mail, and IIUM Confessions.
- **User-Generated Content (UGC):** These are open systems with flexible topics that rely heavily on audience contributions. Examples include Wikis, YouTube, and Facebook.

**2. Development of Multimedia Research Tools**

- **Purpose:** These tools offer innovative techniques to link qualitative and quantitative elements, creating quick, cost-efficient, and attractive research methods. They aim to increase respondent involvement, provide a greater variety of methods, and yield better data quality.
- **Key Tools:**
    - Custom Sliders and Drag & Drop interfaces.
    - Avatars.
    - Hotspots / Heatmaps (visualizing where users click or focus).
    - Mouse Tracking (analyzing user movement patterns).

**3. Livestreaming Platforms and Monetization**

- **Major Platforms:** Facebook Watch, YouTube Live, Twitch, and TikTok.
- **Monetization Strategies:**
    - **Advertising:** Short ad breaks paid by sponsors (e.g., $2-5 per 1000 views), where creators get a percentage cut (e.g., 70% on YouTube).
    - **Micro-donations:** Small payments or tips from viewers, such as YouTube Super Stickers, Facebook Stars, Twitch Bits, and TikTok Gifts.
    - **Subscriptions:** Paid monthly by viewers (e.g., YouTube Join, Twitch Subs).
    - **Endorsements:** Sponsorships arranged through tools like the Creator Marketplace.
- **Criteria for Monetization:** Creators generally need a minimum number of views, followers (~1k-10k minimum), total watch time, and a mature account age.

**4. Livestreaming Tools**

- **StreamYard:** Known for being user-friendly with built-in features for banners and comments, though the free account is limited.
- **OBS (Open Broadcaster Software):** A completely free and fully functional tool, but it comes with a high learning curve for users.

**5. Pros and Cons of Online Multimedia**

- **Advantages:** It provides valuable resources, allows non-technical users easy access to web publishing, enables global collaboration on single documents, and acts as a flexible tool with no predetermined structure.
- **Disadvantages:** It suffers from a lack of security (passwords can be hacked), has been linked to recent crime cases, cannot be accessed without an internet connection, and can lead to confusion during collaboration if content is not properly monitored.
